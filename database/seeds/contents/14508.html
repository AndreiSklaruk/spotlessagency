Every person who used to work in real estate business knows that empty property is more difficult to sell than staged one. Why does it happen? The answer is apparent. No matter how professional photos of the empty property are it is impossible to learn many important facts from them.

  • First of all, it is the actual size of the room. Most of the people looking for a home for sale first look through the listings. There is hardly anyone who starts reading the adjusting material, usually it happens only when some of the photos have interested a potential buyer. The photo of the empty room will never show its actual size as it is almost impossible to take such a photo. In any case, the impression about it will differ from the one you will get if you see it in reality.
  • Secondly, the room potential is unknown when you see only bare walls. A photo of a non-staged room does not shed light on the fact what type of the room it is, where it is located and how it can be used.
  • Thirdly, on such photos it is impossible to see how light it is and whether you will get any sunshine at all there. No wonder so many buyers even do not pay attention to the empty homes for sale.
